Melty Mozz & Mushroom Panini

Description:

If you’ve ever loved a hot, crisp panini sandwich in your life, you’ll go wild for this one. You’ll swipe savory garlic mayo over sourdough slices, fill with herby sautéed mushrooms, then top with easy-melting mozzarella before it hits a hot pan for that ultra-cheese-stretchy finish everyone’s always photographing (hint, hint!). Nobody likes a lonely sammie, though—pair yours with crispy roasted potato wedges to complete this hearty vegetarian meal that simply can’t be beat!

Ingredients:

  • Potatoes (12oz)
  • Yellow Onion
  • Fresh Mozzarella
  • Mushroom Stock Concentrate
  • Button Mushrooms
  • Garlic (2 cloves)
  • Dried Thyme
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Cooking Oil
  • Mayo
  • Butter
  • Ketchup
  1. Adjust rack to middle position and preheat oven to 425 degrees. Wash and dry produce. Cut potatoes into ½-inch-thick wedges. Peel and finely chop garlic. Trim and thinly slice mushrooms (skip this step if your mushrooms are pre-sliced!). Halve, peel, and finely dice half the onion (all for 4 servings). Thinly slice mozzarella into rounds.

  2. Toss potatoes on a baking sheet with a drizzle of oil, salt, and pepper. Roast on middle rack until golden brown and crispy, 20-25 minutes. Meanwhile, in a small bowl, combine 2 TBSP mayonnaise (4 TBSP for 4 servings) and a pinch of garlic (you’ll use the rest later). Season with salt and pepper.

  3. Heat a drizzle of oil in a large, preferably nonstick, pan over medium-high heat. Add onion; cook, stirring, until softened, 2 minutes. Add another drizzle of oil, mushrooms, ½ tsp dried thyme (1 tsp for 4 servings), salt, and pepper; cook, stirring, until veggies are browned and tender, 5-7 minutes.

  4. Add stock concentrate, remaining garlic, and 2 TBSP water (4 TBSP for 4 servings). Cook, stirring, until garlic is fragrant and mixture is saucy, 3 minutes. Turn off heat; transfer to a medium bowl. Wash out pan.

  5. Evenly spread half the bread slices with garlic mayo. Top with mushroom mixture, mozzarella, and remaining bread slices to form sandwiches. In pan used for veggies, melt 1 TBSP butter over medium-high heat. (For 4 servings, you may need to work in batches, adding more butter if needed.) Add sandwiches; cook, pressing down occasionally with a spatula, until bread is golden brown and cheese melts, 2-3 minutes per side. (Cover pan with a lid during the last minute of cooking for extra-melty cheese!) Transfer to a cutting board.

  6. Halve panini on a diagonal. Divide panini between plates along with potato wedges. If you’ve got some on hand, serve with ketchup on the side for dipping.
