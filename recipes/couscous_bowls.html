Garlicky Turkish Couscous Bowls

Lemony couscous is the ideal base for this hearty, wholesome, vegetarian meal. It's topped with spiced roasted vegetables—like crispy-edged broccoli and tender tomatoes—and a dollop of creamy hummus, making a delicious bounty in a bowl! A drizzle of a garlicky lemon sauce completes the dish with a citrusy, zesty punch.

Ingredients:

  • Broccoli
  • Garlic Powder
  • Lemon
  • Mushroom Stock Concentrate
  • Tomato
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Cooking Oil
  • Couscous
  • Feta Cheese (1/2 Cup)
  • Sour Cream (1.5 TBPS)
  • Butter
  • Turkish Spice Blend (1TPSB)

Steps:

  1. Adjust rack to middle position and preheat oven to 425 degrees. Wash and dry produce. Cut broccoli into bite-size pieces. Cut tomatoes lengthwise into quarters. Zest and quarter lemon.

  2. In a large bowl, gently toss broccoli and tomatoes with a large drizzle of oil, stock concentrate, Turkish Spice Blend, half the garlic powder (you'll use the rest later), a small pinch of salt, and pepper. Transfer veggies to a baking sheet and roast on middle rack until browned and tender, 15-20 minutes.

  3. While veggies roast, melt 2 TBSP butter (4 TBSP for 4 servings) in a small pot over medium-high heat. Add couscous; cook, stirring occasionally, until toasted, 1-2 minutes. Stir in 1½ cups water (2½ cups for 4) and a big pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a low simmer. Cook until couscous is tender, 8-10 minutes.

  4. Meanwhile, in a small bowl, combine sour cream with juice from two lemon wedges (four wedges for 4 servings). Add water 1 tsp at a time until mixture reaches a drizzling consistency. Season with salt to taste.

  5. Once couscous is done, stir in a pinch of lemon zest; fluff with a fork. Divide couscous between bowls and top with veggies and hummus. Drizzle garlicky lemon sauce over veggies and serve with remaining lemon wedges on the side.
